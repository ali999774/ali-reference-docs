Zara and Marco: two layers of design authority
Most studios treat design as one role. Milestone Studio splits it: Zara extracts intent (what should this feel like?), Marco executes it into tokens (how does that get built?). Without this split, Marco guesses and produces AI slop. The two-phase approach is the difference between a site that feels designed and one that feels generated.
Dev Kumar outranks Elena — but not on paper
Dev Kumar earns less and has a junior title. But he holds absolute blocking power over Elena — the highest-paid non-lead. This is gate authority: you don't need a senior title to hold a gate. In real firms, QA engineers who understand this dynamic become politically important.
Marco has authority without a report line
Priya and Tyler don't report to Marco — they report to Jordan. But they cannot override Marco's design tokens. His authority is enforced through the code itself, not through management. Real designers who understand this build systems that are self-enforcing.
Jordan is the only person who holds authority twice
Act 1: scaffold architect. Act 2: integration authority — the only person with a full view of all parallel work. This double role is why Jordan is the highest-paid engineer and why Act 2 uses the most powerful model.
